## Overview

This repository presents a fully implementable **climate-cooling and ecosystem-restoration model** designed for semi-arid regions, rocky deserts, and regosol-type degraded lands.

The target is not pure deep dune desert such as the central Sahara.  
The main target is land where mineral structure, shallow soil, rocky dust, or regosol substrate remains, and where biological recovery may be possible if the biological operating system of soil is restored.

This model integrates:

1. **humus-based soil creation**
2. **soil microbiome seeding**: bacteria, fungi, and decomposers
3. **multi-species vegetation**: weeds, legumes, tubers, and herbs
4. **evaporative cooling** through sprinkler systems
5. **continuous mist stabilization** using 5–20 μm droplets
6. **AI environmental control** for soil moisture, humidity, leaf wilt, night temperature, and microbial respiration

This paper is not merely a conceptual proposal.  
It presents a **Level-3 actionable engineering design** with implementation steps, technical parameters, environmental constraints, and feedback-control loops for immediate pilot deployment.

## 1. Background

Semi-arid regions and rocky degraded lands are expanding and deteriorating due to:

- soil sterilization, meaning loss of microbial communities
- declining organic matter
- extreme diurnal temperature swings
- evaporation exceeding precipitation
- vegetation collapse causing further radiative heating
- soil erosion and wind erosion
- loss of water-retention capacity

Semi-arid regions and rocky deserts are different from hyper-arid dune deserts.

If mineral structure, shallow soil, rocky dust, microtopography, groundwater access, or feasible external water supply remains, biological soil restoration may become possible.

## 2. Target Regions and Excluded Regions

### Suitable Target Regions

This model is suitable for:

- semi-arid deserts
- rocky deserts
- regosol barren land
- post-fire degraded landscapes
- degraded steppe zones
- land where organic matter and water have been lost but mineral substrate remains

### Unsuitable or Difficult Regions

This model is not suitable for:

- deep dune-type hyper-arid sand deserts
- shifting aeolian sand fields
- land with almost no rootable mineral structure
- regions without feasible water pipeline, desalination, or reclaimed-water access

This is not a proposal to green every desert on Earth in the same way.  
It is a staged model that begins with recoverable semi-arid, rocky, and degraded steppe regions.

## 4. Technical Specifications: Level-3 Implementation Design

### 4.1 Humus-Based Soil Layer

The initial soil layer may use the following blend.

| Component | Ratio | Function |
|---|---:|---|
| Shredded organic waste | 50% | Municipal, agricultural, and forest-derived organic matter; base material for humus |
| Biochar | 10% | Increases water retention and cation exchange capacity |
| Desert sand / rocky dust | 30% | Mineral substrate |
| Microbial starter | 10% | Reboots the soil biological system |

For the initial seeding layer, approximately **120–180 tons per hectare** of this humus blend is required.  
The assumed depth is approximately **2–3 cm**.

---

### 4.2 Microbiome Starter Formula

Minimum microbial quantities are estimated as follows.

| Type | Minimum Amount |
|---|---:|
| Nitrogen-fixing bacteria | ≥ 10⁸ CFU/ha |
| Mycorrhizal fungi | ≥ 10⁷ CFU/ha |
| Cellulose decomposers | ≥ 10⁸ CFU/ha |
| Actinomycetes | ≥ 10⁷ CFU/ha |

Delivery may be through slurry or solid inoculum.

Microbiome seeding is not simply fertilization.  
It is the rebooting of the soil biological operating system.

---

### 4.3 Multi-Species Vegetation System

Monoculture planting is vulnerable to drought, disease, pests, and temperature fluctuation.

For that reason, this model uses a multi-species mixture.

#### 1. Fast-Growing Weeds and Grasses: 40%

Primary functions are ground coverage, soil fixation, and initial shading.

Examples:

- Naginata-gaya
- *Digitaria sanguinalis*
- African perennial heat-tolerant grasses

#### 2. Legumes: 25%

Primary functions are nitrogen fixation and soil fertility recovery.

Examples:

- Cowpea (*Vigna unguiculata*)
- *Tephrosia* species
- desert-adapted Fabaceae

#### 3. Tubers and Root Crops: 20%

Primary functions are water storage, heat tolerance, and underground biomass formation.

Examples:

- cassava
- sweet potato
- desert lily lineages

#### 4. Herbs: 15%

Primary functions are pest resilience, microbiome stimulation, and biodiversity enhancement.

Examples:

- rosemary
- thyme
- mint
- lavender

Total seed density is approximately **18–30 kg per hectare**.

## 5. Water Infrastructure

### 5.1 Sprinkler Cooling System

Sprinklers primarily form the evaporative cooling loop.

| Parameter | Target Range |
|---|---:|
| Droplet size | 50–200 μm |
| Water rate | 0.8–1.4 mm/day |
| Pump pressure | 1.5–3.0 bar |
| Coverage per head | 8–12 m radius |

The purpose is not irrigation alone.  
The purpose is to directly remove heat from the ground surface and air.

---

### 5.2 Mist Stabilization System

Mist maintains humidity and protects microorganisms.

| Parameter | Target Range |
|---|---:|
| Droplet size | 5–20 μm |
| Night cycle | 00:00–05:00 |
| Daytime humidity maintenance | approximately 30 min per hour |
| Feature | high evaporation efficiency due to large surface-to-volume ratio |

Fine mist suppresses drying of the soil surface and helps prevent microbial die-off.

## 7. Implementation Steps

### STEP 1: Ground Preparation

- Remove large stones.
- Lightly till the top 10–15 cm.
- Spread a 2–3 cm humus layer.
- Apply 2–3 mm of pre-watering.

### STEP 2: Microbiome Activation

- Apply microbial slurry at dusk.
- Maintain humidity above 30% for the first 72 hours.
- Begin nightly mist cycles immediately.

### STEP 3: Seed Broadcasting

- Broadcast seeds uniformly.
- Apply light straw mulch at approximately 300–500 kg/ha.
- Start reduced sprinkler operation.

### STEP 4: Temperature and Moisture Control

- Begin sprinkler and mist hybrid operation.
- Use AI-enabled predictive cooling.

### STEP 5: Root Zone Expansion

After approximately 45–70 days, monitor for:

- possible 150–350% increase in soil water-retention capacity
- possible 20–35% reduction in water use
- expansion of root zones and microbial zones

### STEP 6: Climate Feedback Initiation

When vegetation reaches approximately 40–60 cm height, the following effects may begin:

- reduced surface radiative heat flux
- increased humidity and convective uplift
- higher cloud-formation probability
- regional cooling and moisture stabilization

## 9. Limitations and Precautions

This model has limitations.

- It is not suitable for deep dune-type hyper-arid deserts.
- Initial water infrastructure is required.
- Large-scale deployment requires political coordination.
- Pilot sites should begin at 10–50 ha or smaller.
- Water sources, salinity, local ecosystems, land ownership, and resident consent must be handled carefully.
- To avoid invasive-species risk, locally appropriate native or adapted species must be selected.

This model is not a universal solution for greening all deserts.

It is a staged model that begins with recoverable land and expands through observation and feedback.
